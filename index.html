About

I am a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering at The Pennsylvania State University, advised by Prof. Christopher D. Rahn. My dissertation, "Fault Diagnostics and Mitigation in Lithium-Ion Battery Packs After an Electric Vehicle Accident," was defended in February 2026; I graduate in May 2026.

My research bridges control theory and energy systems — developing algorithms that help first responders assess EV battery safety after crashes, and enabling the safe extraction of stranded energy from damaged packs. I have collaborated with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) and Idaho National Laboratory.

Previously, I was an Intern at Tesla (Aug–Dec 2024), working with the Cell Lifetime Modeling team.

Research Agenda

My future work focuses on robust models and control strategies for next-generation energy systems, as well as advancing theoretical research in control theory.
